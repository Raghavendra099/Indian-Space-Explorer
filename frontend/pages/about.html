About ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was established in 1969 with a singular vision: harness space technology for national development. Today, it is one of the world's six largest space agencies.

From placing India's first satellite Aryabhata in orbit in 1975, to landing near the Moon's south pole with Chandrayaan-3 in 2023, ISRO has consistently achieved the extraordinary — at a fraction of what comparable missions cost elsewhere.

Mangalyaan reached Mars on its very first attempt, for less than the budget of the film Gravity. Aditya-L1 now watches the Sun from 1.5 million km away. Gaganyaan will soon carry Indian astronauts to orbit.
